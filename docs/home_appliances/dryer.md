This technology allows them to use **significantly less electricity** than traditional electric resistance dryers, leading to increased focus on energy efficiency standards globally.

## âš¡ How It Works: Electro-Mechanical and Safety Systems

The functionality of a dryer relies on three primary electrical systems: **Heating**, **Motion**, and **Safety/Control**.

### I. Air Circulation and Motion
1.  **Motor:** An **electric motor** is the central power source. In many designs, a single motor powers both the **drum (via a belt)** and the **blower fan (via a drive shaft)**.
2.  **Drum:** The motor-driven drum tumbles the clothes, ensuring uniform exposure to the heated air.
3.  **Blower Fan:** This fan circulates air. In **vented dryers**, it pulls ambient air in, pushes it over the heating element, through the drum, and out the exhaust vent.

### II. Heating Mechanisms
* **Electric Resistance Dryers (Vented/Condensing):** Use a high-resistance **Nichrome coil heating element** (a major resistive load, often $240\ \text{V}$) to generate heat via the Joule effect.
* **Gas Dryers (Vented):** Use an electric **igniter** (a glow bar) to ignite a gas burner. The primary electricity consumption is for the motor, fan, and igniter, not the heat generation itself.
* **Heat Pump Dryers (Ventless):** The system's compressor and motor run a refrigeration cycle. This cycle serves two functions: **dehumidifying** the air after it leaves the drum, and **reusing the latent heat** released during condensation to reheat the air before it returns to the drum.

### III. Safety and Control
Electrical safety components are mandatory due to the high operating temperatures and power loads:

* **Thermostats:** The dryer uses multiple **thermostats** to control operating temperature. The **cycling thermostat** maintains the selected temperature range by switching the heating element on and off.
* **Thermal Fuse:** This is a **one-time, non-resettable safety device** wired in series with the heating element. If the cycling thermostat fails and the temperature exceeds a dangerously high threshold (e.g., due to a clogged vent), the thermal fuse opens the circuit permanently, preventing fire.
* **Moisture Sensor/Timer:**
    * **Timer-Based:** Uses a simple clock circuit that runs until the set time expires.
    * **Moisture Sensor:** Two metal strips inside the drum detect the electrical **resistance** of the clothes. Wet clothes have low resistance (conduct electricity better); dry clothes have high resistance. The control board monitors this resistance to determine the end of the cycle.
